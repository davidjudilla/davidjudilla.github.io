How I Got Started
I like this story because it's a corner stone for how I've gotten to where I am in my career.
During my first internship I was taking a Caltrain from San Francisco to San Jose to visit my family. So I walk onto the first floor of the train and ask a husky looking guy, around his late 20s, if I could take a seat next to him. He obliges. As I sit down, he's in the middle of putting an Uber sticker on the lid of his laptop. I condescedingly think in my head, "This dude can't like being an Uber driver that much". A few moments after he finished slapping his sticker on, he turns to me and asks, "So what do you do?", in a geniune and non-condescending manner. I quickly tell him about my internship at a small startup in SoMa, in a slightly full of it tone as though not everyone on this train had a job in tech.
He begins to tell me how great it is that I got an internship before graduation and how he just got his job as an engineer at Uber. I swear I must of had the stupidest look on my face. For the rest of the ride, we talked about some of the tools we used at work, like how my company was still used JSP pages to serve up their site. I continued to pick his brain, learning about the importance of design patterns, so that you can understand multiple approaches to problems and be able to discuss them with other engineers. He told me about React and some of the other tools that were coming out at the time. I was simply inspired. I wanted to be this guy. To be so well spoken about what it's like being a software developer in the city. From there I followed every web dev/js subreddit I could find. I read so many Medium articles on how to get started with React and took online workshops like Frontend Masters. I got lost in it all and I've loved every second of it to this day. All just so that I could be the best developer I could be.
